What your FPS number means

30 FPS is the floor for playable; moving to 60 FPS is the single most noticeable jump in smoothness, while gains beyond 120 FPS are subtle. At 60 FPS each frame has only about a 16.7 ms budget, so anything that stalls the frame shows up as a stutter.

FPSHow it feels
< 30Visible stutter; hard to aim or scroll smoothly.
30–45Playable; fine for casual and turn-based games.
60Smooth; the comfortable target for most games.
90–120Ultra smooth; needs a 120 Hz+ screen and a strong GPU.

Will MuMu Player run smoothly on your PC?

MuMu Player needs at least a 4-core CPU with VT enabled, 4 GB of RAM and a GTX 950-class GPU. Most stutter comes from VT being switched off, too little CPU or RAM allocated to the instance, or the wrong graphics rendering mode — the MuMu performance and high-FPS guide walks through each fix step by step.

Quick real-world checks

  • Browser caps at 60 FPS but your GPU is strong? That is your monitor refresh rate or browser throttling, not the emulator limit.
  • Smooth in the browser yet laggy in MuMu? Check that VT (virtualization) is enabled in BIOS — it is the most common cause.
  • Multi-instance stutter: lower the cores and RAM per instance, or run fewer instances at once.
  • Long load stutters on an old mechanical drive? Install MuMu Player on an SSD.
  • GPU shows as SwiftShader or software? Hardware acceleration is off; enable it for an accurate reading and better game performance.

Frequently asked questions

How does this FPS test work?
It uses the browser requestAnimationFrame callback to count how many frames render each second. All of it runs locally in your browser; nothing is uploaded.
Why is my FPS capped at 60?
Browsers synchronise drawing to your display refresh rate. A 60 Hz screen caps the browser near 60 FPS even when your GPU could draw more.
Does a high browser FPS mean MuMu games will be smooth?
It is a rough proxy for device performance. Actual emulator smoothness also depends on VT, the CPU and RAM you allocate, and the graphics rendering mode.
What are the MuMu Player minimum requirements?
Windows 7 or newer, a 4-core CPU such as the i5-7500 with VT enabled, a GTX 950-class GPU and 4 GB of RAM, per the official requirements.
Why is MuMu Player stuttering?
Most often VT is disabled, the instance has too few cores or too little RAM, or the graphics rendering mode does not suit your GPU. The linked performance guide covers each fix.
Can I reach 120 FPS in MuMu Player?
Some games support a high-FPS mode. You also need a 120 Hz or faster display and a capable GPU to actually see it.
Does this tool upload any data?
No. The FPS measurement and device read-out run entirely in your browser and are never sent to a server.
My GPU shows as software or SwiftShader — what does that mean?
Your browser is not using hardware acceleration. Turn it on in browser settings for an accurate reading and smoother emulator performance.
